Mortgage

Mortgages are long term loans that are taken out in order to buy either a property or land. These loans are then repaid over a period of up to 35 years, with interest rates. As mortgages are one of the most expensive financial loans taken out, it is important to get professional consultation and advice to help find the correct mortgage for you.

Here at Royle Standard, we can assist you by introducing you to a professional (IFA) Independent Financial Advisor for advice for the correct investment product that may meet your needs.

  • Royle Standard must point out that various mortgages have advantages and disadvantages associated with them.
  • You also need to be aware that with both a ‘repayment mortgage’ and an ‘interest only mortgage’ it is your responsibility to ensure that you maintain any such repayment vehicle for the term of the mortgage otherwise you may not be able to repay the debt at the end of the term, thus resulting in possible repossession of your home.

Key Terms:

  • Rest: lenders may calculate the interest rate Daily, Monthly or Annually according to their product range.
  • Term: period that a mortgage/re-mortgage has been arranged to be repaid over.

Types of Mortgages

Interest on the loan and part of the capital is paid to the lender in the form of regular monthly instalments. In the early years, most of the payments are interest. The capital is paid off slowly in the latter years. The size of the loan therefore gradually decreases during the term of the mortgage.

Advantages:

  • You pay less interest overall
  • Lower interest rates
  • You will own your home at the end of the mortgage term

Disadvantages:

  • Higher monthly repayment

This type of mortgage’s aim is to provide a lump sum for you to pay off the mortgage at the end of the mortgage term. Interest on the loan is paid to the lender each month, while a cash sum is built up using an investment vehicle.

Examples:

  • Endowment
  • Private Pension Plan
  • Individual Savings Account (ISA)
  • Unit Trust
  • Single Investment Bond
  • Saving Plan

Advantages:

  • Lower monthly payments
  • More flexibility to choose where your money goes
  • You could make a profit if your investments perform well

Disadvantages:

  • More expensive overall
  • More complicated to look after
  • Higher risk of bad repayment performance

Monthly payments of Interest or Capital & Interest. The advantage of a fixed rate mortgage is that the interest payment will not change during the fixed term which can typically be anything from two to five years. This means that you can manage your household budget, knowing that your biggest monthly outgoing is not about to increase suddenly.

There are currently some very good deals available for fixed-rate mortgages, especially for first-time buyers.

With a capped rate mortgage, you will know the maximum amount of your interest payment that you will have to pay. Interest rates for this type of mortgage have a maximum rate but no minimum rate. If interest rates rise above your capped rate you pay nothing more, but you also benefit at lower rates as your interest repayments are calculated on this lower rate.

With these mortgages the interest rate is variable (it can rise or fall) with a given discount applied to that rate. The discount period exists for a predetermined period of time; the shorter the time period then generally the bigger the discount and vice versa.

Standard variable rate mortgages are when the payments go up and down in line with the intertest rates. Many lenders offer a discounted period within this method. However, there may be penalties for early repayment.

Base rate trackers are when the interest rates is tracked to the Bank of England rate. Therefore, the payments tend to go up and down in line with the interest rates. This type of mortgage is starting to be offered by many lenders and can also be offered with a discounted period; a discount is given off the current Bank of England rate for a certain period

What is Buy to Let?
Buy to Let is when there is a property you would like to purchase in order to rent out to residents. This is often offered as a business loans instead of residential mortgages. However, for this type of mortgage, the rental income taken into account when the lender assesses affordability criteria.

What is Consumer Buy to Let?
Consumer Buy to Let is regulated as residential mortgage. This is normally for those who are accidental, or non-professional landlords and this type of mortgage can offer the consumers protection to the people letting their homes.

Can I get a Buy to Let mortgage?
Yes. Our Mortgage Advisors will find you the most competitive mortgage.

How much can I borrow?
Lenders will typically advance a maximum of 80% of the property price. Some lenders say the rental income must be at least 125% of the mortgage payment.

Is there a Buy-to-Let Interest Rate?
Most borrowers for Buy-to-Lets prefer Fixed or Capped rate mortgage so that they can plan income and outgoings better. At one time lenders charged commercial rates on loans taken out to buy property to let. Since more lenders have entered the market, who are prepared to lend for Buy to Let properties. So, interest rates are normal only slightly higher than ordinary home loans. With the present low interest rates more and more people are attracted to enter the Buy to Let market.

More than one property?
Some lenders will only lend on one property; others will limit you to 10 or more, and some may have a limit on the overall amount you can borrow, e.g. £1 million.

What about Tax Relief?
You can offset interest payments on your mortgage against tax on rental income, along with other expenses such as agents' fees and maintenance costs. You should confirm existing tax legislation with your tax advisor.

How does an offshore mortgage differ?
The mortgage is provided by a bank based in an offshore location such as the Isle of Man. This means that if you belong to any of the groups above, you can benefit from offshore tax planning. The principal advantage of an offshore mortgage is that the payment of interest (but not capital) out of non-UK income is not regarded as a taxable remittance to the UK.
The mortgage interest can also be set against any taxable UK rental income, reducing the UK tax liability for non-UK resident landlords.

Please note that tax planning is a complex area and you should seek specialist advice when considering the use of one of these products.
